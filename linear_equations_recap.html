2Finding Coordinates

Coordinates are written as (x, y) pairs that represent points on a graph.

Finding coordinates from an equation:

Given: y = 2x - 1

To find coordinates, choose x-values and solve for y:

  • If x = 0: y = 2(0) - 1 = -1 ‚Üí Point: (0, -1)
  • If x = 1: y = 2(1) - 1 = 1 ‚Üí Point: (1, 1)
  • If x = 3: y = 2(3) - 1 = 5 ‚Üí Point: (3, 5)

3Understanding y = mx + b

y = mx + b

What it represents:

This is the slope-intercept form of a linear equation. It directly shows you the slope and y-intercept of the line.

Breaking it down:

  • y = output/dependent variable
  • m = slope (rise over run)
  • x = input/independent variable
  • b = y-intercept (where line crosses y-axis)

Example: y = 3x - 4

‚Ä¢ Slope (m) = 3 (line rises 3 units for every 1 unit right)

‚Ä¢ Y-intercept (b) = -4 (line crosses y-axis at (0, -4))

‚Ä¢ x and y are variables representing any point on the line

4Finding the Slope

The slope measures how steep a line is and its direction.

Slope = m = (y‚ÇÇ - y‚ÇÅ)/(x‚ÇÇ - x‚ÇÅ) = rise/run

Example: Find slope between points (1, 2) and (4, 8)

m = (8 - 2)/(4 - 1) = 6/3 = 2

The slope is 2, meaning the line rises 2 units for every 1 unit to the right.

From two points:

Use the slope formula with any two points on the line

From equation:

In y = mx + b form, m is the slope

Example: y = -2x + 5 ‚Üí slope = -2

7X- and Y-Intercepts

Y-Intercept

Where the line crosses the y-axis

To find: Set x = 0 and solve for y

Point form: (0, b)

X-Intercept

Where the line crosses the x-axis

To find: Set y = 0 and solve for x

Point form: (a, 0)

Example: Find intercepts of 2x + 3y = 12

Y-intercept: Set x = 0

2(0) + 3y = 12 ‚Üí 3y = 12 ‚Üí y = 4

Y-intercept: (0, 4)

X-intercept: Set y = 0

2x + 3(0) = 12 ‚Üí 2x = 12 ‚Üí x = 6

X-intercept: (6, 0)

8Three Forms of Linear Equations

Slope-Intercept Form

y = mx + b

When to use: When you know slope and y-intercept

Shows: Slope (m) and y-intercept (b) directly

Point-Slope Form

y - y‚ÇÅ = m(x - x‚ÇÅ)

When to use: When you know slope and one point

Shows: Slope (m) and point (x‚ÇÅ, y‚ÇÅ)

Standard Form

Ax + By = C

When to use: For finding intercepts easily

Shows: Both variables on left side

Converting Between Forms:

Example: Convert y - 3 = 2(x + 1) to slope-intercept form

1 Start: y - 3 = 2(x + 1)
2 Distribute: y - 3 = 2x + 2
3 Add 3: y = 2x + 5
4 Result: y = 2x + 5 (slope-intercept form)

9How to Graph Linear Equations

Method 1: Using Intercepts

1 Find x-intercept (set y = 0)
2 Find y-intercept (set x = 0)
